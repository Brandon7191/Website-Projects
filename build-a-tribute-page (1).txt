                <h2> Here's a timeline of his life</h2>
                <ul>
                  <li><strong>1891-</strong><p>Pedro Albizu Campos is born in Ponce, Puerto Rico, to a family of mixed African and Basque heritage.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1912-</strong><p>Albizu Campos graduates from the University of Vermont with a degree in chemical engineering, becoming the first Puerto Rican to graduate from an Ivy League university.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1917-</strong><p>Albizu Campos enlists in the U.S. Army and serves in World War I. He is commissioned as a second lieutenant and awarded the Croix de Guerre for bravery.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1921-</strong><p>Albizu Campos returns to Puerto Rico and begins practicing law.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1924-</strong><p>Albizu Campos becomes involved in the Puerto Rican nationalist movement and is elected president of the Puerto Rican Nationalist Party (PNP)</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1930-</strong><p>Albizu Campos is arrested and imprisoned for seditious conspiracy after leading a nationalist uprising in Puerto Rico.</p>
</li>
                  <li><strong>1936-</strong><p>Albizu Campos is released from prison and resumes his leadership of the PNP.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1940-</strong><p>Albizu Campos is re-arrested and imprisoned, this time for advocating Puerto Rican independence and challenging the authority of the U.S. government.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1947-</strong><p>Albizu Campos is released from prison due to deteriorating health, but he is still closely monitored by U.S. authorities.</p>
</li>
                  <li><strong>1950-</strong><p>The Nationalist Party organizes a series of uprisings in Puerto Rico, known as the Jayuya Uprising, the Utuado Uprising, and the San Juan Nationalist Revolt, with Albizu Campos as the overall commander. The uprisings are unsuccessful, and Albizu Campos is arrested and imprisoned again.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1954-</strong><p>Albizu Campos is released from prison for a second time, but his health has deteriorated further due to the harsh conditions of his imprisonment.</p></li>
                  <li><strong>1964-</strong><p>Pedro Albizu Campos dies at the age of 72 from complications related to his imprisonment and poor health.</p></li>
                  </ul>
